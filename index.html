My name is Rachel Bailey and I am a PhD candidate in the Mathematics department at the University of Connecticut. My research interests are in orthogonal polynomials, stochastic processes and operator theory. In particular, I am interested in exploring what properties of orthogonal polynomials are preserved under perturbations of their corresponding linear functionals. I am also curious about the the roles of orthogonal polynomials in semigroup theory and their connections with stochastic processes. I am very passionate about teaching mathematics and I strive to provide information to students in ways that encourage curiosity, build students' confidence and develop their problem solving skills.
